About Me

My passion for providing unique and individualized speech, language and swallowing therapy has led me to open my own practice after 11 years of working as a therapist in acute care; acute rehab; skilled nursing; adult day care; pediatric outpatient; and adult Neurorehabilitation outpatient settings. I worked for 7 years at Mills-Peninsula Health Services in San Mateo and Burlingame treating and evaluating both pediatric and adult patients. In 2008 I earned a Master's in Business Administration specializing in Healthcare Management. For the last four years I enjoyed working as the lead Outpatient Speech Language Pathologist for the Neurorehabilitation program at Stanford University Hospital and as a Per Diem for Inpatient Speech Therapy at Stanford Hospital.

"We first met Megan seven years ago when our son was 2 ½. He didn’t speak at all and qualified to start receiving speech therapy. Megan came over to our home twice a week and worked with him in a caring, devoted, fun way. She first taught him sign language and he was able to communicate his needs to us within a short period of time. Soon after this he was making sounds and words because of the many creative techniques that Megan used with him each session. We marveled at all of the innovative, unique methods such as when she put spreadable cheese on his lower lip and then ask him to scrape it off with his teeth to teach him the “f” sound rather than the “th” sound he was making. She carefully determined the things that appealed to him and integrated his “likes” into the structure of her sessions. Every single session, she would bring something new with her to teach him sounds and words. Her approach to him was consistently purposeful, methodical and fun. Our son looked forward to every session with Megan. To say that we think she’s amazing would be an understatement.
After he aged out of GGRC we went through the public school route to try and have his speech therapy continue. We were denied and if it weren’t for Megan attending a district meeting and advocating for our son, he would not have received the remaining two years that he was allowed. In a professional sense, we can’t say enough. This is nothing, however, compared to how our family has gotten to know Megan personally. She feels like a member of our own family and makes all of us feel the same way. We felt this connection with her instantly. She attends our son’s soccer games, our daughter’s dance concerts and supports our family in every way possible because she is remarkably warm, caring, and genuine. Our lives are enriched by knowing Megan and we absolutely adore her."
